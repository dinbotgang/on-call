# On Call — Branding Notes

## Name
**On Call** — clean, immediate, implies availability and readiness.
- Tagline ideas:
  - *"Work when you want. Get help when you need it."*
  - *"The job's right there. So are you."*
  - *"Skilled help, on demand."*
  - *"Whatever you need done — someone nearby is ready."*

## Vibe
- **Modern but approachable** — not corporate, not trashy
- **Action-oriented** — this is about doing things, getting things done
- **Local & real** — authentic workers with real faces, not stock photos
- Feels like: Uber + Hinge + Field & Stream had a baby

## Color Direction (TBD)
- Primary: Deep blue or slate (trust, reliability) — or —
- Primary: Bold orange/amber (energy, action, construction-adjacent)
- Accent: White + dark gray
- Avoid: Pastel/"startup pink", overly techy neon

## Logo Concepts
- A phone icon with a wrench/tool? Too literal.
- Bold wordmark "ON CALL" in all-caps with a subtle pulse/signal motif
- Or: a location pin with a tool inside
- Keep it simple enough to work as an app icon at 60x60px

## Tone of Voice
- **Direct** — "Book in 60 seconds." not "We're revolutionizing the way you hire."
- **Confident** — trust that workers are vetted and reliable
- **Human** — "Maria, 4.9 ⭐, 2.3 miles away" not "Worker ID #4821"

## Competitor Branding (for reference/differentiation)
- TaskRabbit — blue, friendly, corporate
- Thumbtack — yellow, clean, professional
- Handy — dark, lifestyle-y
- **On Call** should feel faster and more visceral than all of them
