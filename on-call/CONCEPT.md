# On Call — App Concept

> Uber meets Tinder/Hinge — but for any contract work.

---

## Elevator Pitch

**On Call** is a two-sided marketplace app that connects people who need skilled contract work done *right now* with vetted local workers available to take the job. Think Uber's on-demand immediacy fused with Hinge's "swipe to match" discovery experience — but instead of rides or dates, you're booking a bartender, a handyman, a caddy, or a snow removal crew.

---

## The Core Problem

- People need skilled help on short notice and have no easy way to find vetted, nearby workers
- Skilled workers have downtime between gigs and no efficient way to pick up nearby jobs
- Existing platforms (TaskRabbit, Thumbtack) are slow, form-heavy, and designed for scheduled bookings — not immediacy

## User Modes (On Signup)

Users choose one role at signup (switchable later):

### 🔵 Hire Mode — "I need help"
- Browse available workers nearby
- Post a job or swipe on available pros
- Book instantly or schedule ahead
- Pay in-app, rate after

### 🟢 Work Mode — "I'm available"
- Toggle availability on/off (like Uber driver mode)
- Set your skills, radius, hourly rate
- Accept or decline incoming job requests
- Build your profile/reputation

## Core UX Flow

### Hiring a Worker
1. Open app → see nearby available workers (card stack or map view)
2. Swipe / tap to view profile — skills, rating, distance, hourly rate
3. Tap "Book" → instant request sent (like Uber)
4. Worker accepts → ETA shown
5. Job complete → in-app payment + mutual rating

### Finding Work (Worker Side)
1. Toggle "I'm Available" 
2. Incoming job requests appear (like Uber pings)
3. Accept or decline (with reason)
4. Navigate to job via in-app map
5. Mark complete → get paid → rated

## Key Product Pillars

1. **Speed** — from need to booking in under 60 seconds
2. **Trust** — verified profiles, background checks, ratings, photos
3. **Simplicity** — card-based UI, no long forms
4. **Flexibility** — workers set their own rates + availability
5. **Breadth** — anything people need done that isn't a full-time job

## Business Model

| Stream | Detail |
|---|---|
| Platform fee | 15–20% cut of every transaction |
| Worker subscriptions | "Pro" tier for featured placement, better analytics |
| Boost (hiring) | Pay to bump your job to top of worker feed |
| Background check fee | One-time $10–15 charged to workers |
| Cancellation fee | Flat fee for last-minute cancellations (shared with worker) |

---

## Differentiation — The "Hinge Layer"

Unlike Uber (purely transactional), On Call workers have **rich profiles**:
- Photo carousel (working, tools, past jobs)
- Short bio + "Why I do this" prompt
- Specialties + certifications
- Review highlights (like Airbnb "What guests say")
- Response rate + average accept time
- Availability calendar

This builds **trust before the booking** — critical for letting a stranger into your home or on your mountain.
